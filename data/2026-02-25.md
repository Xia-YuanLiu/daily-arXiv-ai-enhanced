### [1] [FastSAM-3D: Real-Time 3D Segment Anything with Sparse Point Prompts](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.18001)
*Wei Zhang,Jian Li,Yiming Chen*

Main category: cs.CV

TL;DR: FastSAM-3D 是一个实时三维点云分割框架，将 SAM 的零样本分割能力扩展至 3D 场景，在保持最优性能的同时实现 15 FPS 的推理速度。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: SAM 在 2D 图像中展现出强大的零样本分割能力，但由于 3D 点云数据具有不规则结构和大规模特性，将其直接扩展至 3D 场景仍面临显著挑战，现有方法推理速度慢，难以满足实时需求。

Method: 提出三项核心设计：① 基于稀疏卷积的轻量级 3D 编码器，高效处理点云数据；② 提示引导注意力机制（prompt-guided attention），实现稀疏点提示与 3D 特征的高效交互；③ 多尺度特征聚合模块，同时捕获局部几何细节与全局语义上下文。

Result: 在 ScanNet、S3DIS 和 nuScenes 三个基准数据集上达到最优（SOTA）分割性能，单 GPU 推理速度为 15 FPS，比现有方法快 5 倍。

Conclusion: FastSAM-3D 成功将 SAM 的交互式分割范式扩展至实时 3D 点云场景，通过轻量化架构与高效注意力机制在精度与速度之间取得良好平衡，具备实际部署潜力。

Abstract: The Segment Anything Model (SAM) has demonstrated remarkable zero-shot segmentation capabilities in 2D images. However, extending SAM to 3D point clouds remains challenging due to the irregular structure and large scale of 3D data. In this paper, we propose FastSAM-3D, a real-time 3D segmentation framework that efficiently processes point cloud data with sparse point prompts. Our method introduces a lightweight 3D encoder based on sparse convolutions and a novel prompt-guided attention mechanism that enables efficient interaction between point prompts and 3D features. We further propose a multi-scale feature aggregation module that captures both local geometric details and global semantic context. Extensive experiments on ScanNet, S3DIS, and nuScenes datasets demonstrate that FastSAM-3D achieves state-of-the-art performance while running at 15 FPS on a single GPU, which is 5x faster than existing methods. Code is available at https://github.com/zhangwei/FastSAM-3D.

### [2] [DiffEdit-3D: Text-Guided 3D Scene Editing via Score Distillation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.18004)
*Maria Garcia,Carlos Rodriguez,Ana Martinez*

Main category: cs.CV

TL;DR: DiffEdit-3D 是一个基于文本引导的3D场景编辑框架，通过多视角分数蒸馏采样损失和注意力定位模块，实现高效、跨视角一致的3D场景编辑。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 现有文本引导3D场景编辑方法存在两大缺陷：跨视角编辑不一致，或需要高昂的逐场景优化代价，缺乏兼顾一致性与效率的解决方案。

Method: 利用预训练2D扩散模型，提出三项核心技术：(1) 多视角分数蒸馏采样损失（MV-SDS），同时约束所有视角的几何与外观一致性；(2) 基于注意力机制的编辑定位模块，自动识别并仅修改3D场景中的相关区域；(3) 渐进式编辑策略，将复杂编辑分解为多步简单的顺序修改。

Result: 在合成与真实3D场景上的实验表明，DiffEdit-3D 生成的编辑结果质量高、视角一致性强，且推理速度比基于优化的方法快3倍。

Conclusion: DiffEdit-3D 有效解决了文本引导3D场景编辑中一致性与效率的矛盾，为直觉式3D内容创作提供了实用可行的新范式。

Abstract: Text-guided 3D scene editing has emerged as a promising direction for intuitive content creation. However, existing methods either produce inconsistent edits across viewpoints or require expensive per-scene optimization. We propose DiffEdit-3D, a framework for consistent and efficient text-guided 3D scene editing. Our method leverages a pre-trained 2D diffusion model and introduces a novel multi-view score distillation sampling (MV-SDS) loss that ensures geometric and appearance consistency across all viewpoints simultaneously. We further propose an attention-based edit localization module that automatically identifies and modifies only the relevant regions of the 3D scene, preserving unedited areas. A progressive editing strategy is employed to handle complex edits by breaking them into simpler sequential modifications. Experiments on both synthetic and real-world 3D scenes demonstrate that DiffEdit-3D produces high-quality, view-consistent edits while being 3x faster than optimization-based alternatives.

### [3] [LongRAG: Extending Retrieval-Augmented Generation to Million-Token Contexts](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.18002)
*Sarah Johnson,Michael Brown,Emily Davis*

Main category: cs.CL

TL;DR: LongRAG 是一个扩展 RAG 能力至百万级 token 上下文的新框架，通过三项核心创新显著提升多文档问答精度。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 现有 RAG 系统难以处理极长上下文场景，在跨数百篇文档的信息检索与综合方面表现不足。

Method: 提出三项关键技术：(1) 文档、段落、句子三级层次化检索机制；(2) 渐进式上下文压缩技术，在保留关键信息的同时将 token 数量压缩 10 倍；(3) 基于检索置信度分数动态调整模型注意力的检索感知注意力机制。

Result: 在 MuSiQue、HotpotQA 及新提出的 LongBench-1M 数据集上，LongRAG 相较现有 RAG 基线在答案准确率上提升 23%，同时保持相当的推理延迟。

Conclusion: LongRAG 有效突破了传统 RAG 在长上下文处理上的瓶颈，为大语言模型在海量文档场景下的知识接地提供了可扩展的解决方案。

Abstract: Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) has become a standard approach for grounding large language models in external knowledge. However, existing RAG systems struggle with extremely long contexts, often failing to retrieve and synthesize information spread across hundreds of documents. We present LongRAG, a novel framework that extends RAG capabilities to handle million-token contexts through three key innovations: (1) a hierarchical retrieval mechanism that operates at document, paragraph, and sentence levels; (2) a progressive context compression technique that preserves salient information while reducing token count by 10x; and (3) a retrieval-aware attention mechanism that dynamically adjusts the model's focus based on retrieval confidence scores. Evaluated on multi-document QA benchmarks including MuSiQue, HotpotQA, and our newly introduced LongBench-1M dataset, LongRAG achieves 23% improvement in answer accuracy over existing RAG baselines while maintaining comparable latency.

### [4] [CodeBridge: Bridging Natural Language and Code Through Bidirectional Translation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.18005)
*Alex Kim,David Park,Jennifer Lee*

Main category: cs.CL

TL;DR: CodeBridge 是一个支持自然语言与代码双向翻译的统一模型，覆盖多种编程语言，并在多个基准测试上达到最优性能。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 自然语言描述与可执行代码之间存在显著鸿沟，现有代码生成模型大多仅支持单向翻译（自然语言→代码），缺乏对代码→自然语言方向的联合建模能力。

Method: 提出 CodeBridge 统一模型，核心包含三点：①通过对比学习构建共享语义空间，对齐自然语言与代码的表示；②引入语法感知解码策略，利用语法约束保证生成代码的句法合法性；③在包含 200 万条对齐代码-描述对的数据集上联合训练双向翻译任务，数据覆盖 Python、Java、JavaScript 和 C++。

Result: 在 HumanEval 上 pass@1 达 89.2%，MBPP 上 pass@1 达 82.7%，并在 CodeXGLUE 代码摘要基准上取得最优结果，全面超越现有方法。

Conclusion: 联合训练双向翻译任务结合共享语义空间与语法感知解码，能有效提升代码生成与代码摘要的综合性能，CodeBridge 为弥合自然语言与代码之间的语义鸿沟提供了统一且高效的解决方案。

Abstract: The gap between natural language descriptions and executable code remains a significant barrier in software development. We present CodeBridge, a unified model for bidirectional translation between natural language and code across multiple programming languages. Unlike existing code generation models that focus primarily on natural language to code translation, CodeBridge jointly trains on both directions, enabling it to generate code from descriptions and produce accurate natural language explanations of code snippets. Our architecture introduces a shared semantic space where both natural language and code representations are aligned through contrastive learning. We also propose a syntax-aware decoding strategy that leverages grammar constraints to ensure generated code is always syntactically valid. Trained on a curated dataset of 2M aligned code-description pairs spanning Python, Java, JavaScript, and C++, CodeBridge achieves new state-of-the-art results on HumanEval (pass@1: 89.2%), MBPP (pass@1: 82.7%), and CodeXGLUE code summarization benchmarks.

### [5] [ReasonAgent: Teaching Language Models to Reason Through Self-Play Reinforcement Learning](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.18003)
*Takeshi Yamamoto,Hiroshi Tanaka,Yuki Sato,Kenji Watanabe*

Main category: cs.AI

TL;DR: 提出ReasonAgent框架，通过自博弈强化学习增强大语言模型的多步推理能力。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 大语言模型在多步数学和逻辑推理任务中能力有限，现有链式思维提示方法缺乏对中间推理步骤的有效监督与纠错机制。

Method: 构建双智能体自博弈机制：提议者（Proposer）生成推理链，批评者（Critic）对其进行评估，两者通过迭代竞争共同优化；同时引入逐步密集奖励塑形技术，在每个推理步骤提供反馈，而非仅在最终答案处给予奖励；整体通过强化学习训练推理策略网络，使模型具备问题分解、中间结果验证及回溯纠错能力。

Result: 在GSM8K、MATH和ARC-Challenge三个基准上，相较于链式思维提示基线，推理准确率提升18%–31%，在需要5步以上推理的复杂问题上提升尤为显著。

Conclusion: 自博弈强化学习结合密集步骤级奖励是提升LLM复杂推理能力的有效范式，ReasonAgent在多步推理场景下展现出显著优势。

Abstract: While large language models have shown impressive capabilities in various tasks, their reasoning abilities remain limited, particularly in multi-step mathematical and logical problems. We introduce ReasonAgent, a framework that enhances LLM reasoning through self-play reinforcement learning. Our approach trains a reasoning policy network that learns to decompose complex problems into manageable sub-steps, verify intermediate results, and backtrack when errors are detected. The key innovation is a dual-agent self-play mechanism where a proposer agent generates reasoning chains and a critic agent evaluates them, with both agents improving through iterative competition. We also introduce a novel reward shaping technique that provides dense feedback at each reasoning step rather than only at the final answer. Experiments on GSM8K, MATH, and ARC-Challenge show that ReasonAgent improves reasoning accuracy by 18-31% over chain-of-thought prompting baselines, with particularly strong gains on problems requiring 5+ reasoning steps.

</details>
